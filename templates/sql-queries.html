Analyzing Internal Team Data & KPIs

The Project

I helped lead and facilitate an Empathy Mapping workshop for the Phoenix Design Thinking & Innovative Collective meetup group.

Purpose

We helped attendees understand what empathy mapping is and how to use it with their teams.

Skills

Presenting to groups, facilitating group discussions, persona creation, empathy mapping, qualitative user analysis

Questions to Answer

My manager asked us to analyze the Marketing Operations team data to help him answer the following questions:

  • What percentage of UX tickets hit published without any points? Is that number increasing or decreasing over time?
  • What is the distribution of points across across each role? (e.g. what percentage of copywriting tickets are small vs medium vs large?)
  • What is the time to market (in progress, ready to published) for each role? How does this change according to ticket size?
  • How do the actual time to market numbers compare to the estimated SLAs documented on the confluence page?

The Prep Work

The data needed to be cleaned up before I could convert it to a SQL database because it contained numerous data errors, as well as a mixture of various data types in certain columns.

Step 1: Clean the Data

To get started, I used the Find All feature to find all of the errors in the data. Once I figured out how to fix each formula to remove the errors it generated, I used the Replace All feature to quickly update hundreds of cells using that same formula.
